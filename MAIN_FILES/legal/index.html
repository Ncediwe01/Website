About Us

Aimed at promoting effective public participation through fighting idleness and disrespect together with communities.

They educate youth on politics and encourages them to take action using political structuring and pick up where the government cannot.

The programme encourages communities to view themselves as a state and mimi precisely the structures of government such as; Virtual Cabinet and Virtual Parliament.

Structure

This is who we are, who we are going to be and what we want to do.

Mission

To establish Virtual States and enroll/harness the untapped skills and developmental potential of the unemployed members of the communities

Vision

To minimize prevalence of idleness and disrespect in the community

V-Parliament

The V-Parliament members are subjected to a series of public participation training programmes offered by the KwaZulu-Natal Legislature’s Public Participation and Petitions unit working closely with the Community and the V-Parliament Operations units

V-Cabinet

Through its Rules, the V-Parliament nominates a Virtual Cabinet responsible for developing and projects aimed at implementing V-Parliament resolutions.

Community Solution Projects

The main weapons used by the V-State to fight idleness. There is a strong conviction that through CSPs, the unemployed do become a resource available to community development urgencies (government, councilors, traditional leaders, religious leaders, NGOs etc) in the work of community development. In the process, they may develop economic ventures for themselves
